Biography

Dana Marie Perino (born May 9, 1972) is an accomplished political analyst, a well-known author, and a former White House Press Secretary. 

She was the 26th press secretary for President George W. Bush from September 14, 2007, until January 20, 2009. Her tenure at the White House was notable. 

After Dee Dee Myers, who held the office during President Bill Clinton‘s term, she became the second woman to hold the position due to her appointment to it. Dana Perino‘s legacy motivates many women who want to change the political and media landscape.

Early Life & Education

Dana Perino, a well-known figure, was born on May 9, 1972, in Evanston, Wyoming. Her early years unfolded in the picturesque city of Denver, Colorado, under the care of her parents, Leo Perino and Jan Perino. Growing up, she shared her childhood with her beloved sister, Angie Perino.

For her education, Perino attended Ponderosa High School in Parker, a suburb of Denver. Following high school, she pursued higher education at Colorado State University, Pueblo, where she obtained a bachelor’s degree in mass communications. 

During her university years, she majored in political science and Spanish, showcasing her diverse academic interests. Dana Perino was actively involved on campus, contributed her skills to the university’s forensics team, and gained practical experience at KTSC-TV, the Rocky Mountain PBS affiliate.

Career

Commencing her journey in the Bush administration in 2002 as the Associate Director of Communications for the White House Council on Environmental Quality, Dana Perino swiftly climbed the ranks. Her dedication and hard work led to her promotion to Deputy Press Secretary in 2005, a role she embraced until March 2007. 

Following the resignation of Tony Snow due to colon cancer treatment, Perino assumed the role of Acting Press Secretary, later officially taking on the position in April 2007. Her tenure continued until January 2009, when Barack Obama assumed the presidency, prompting her departure.

Post her White House tenure, Dana Perino ventured into the media industry, finding a new home at Fox News Channel. Here, she became a political commentator and co-host of the popular show “The Five.” 

Demonstrating her versatility, Dana Perino also showcased her creative prowess by penning a memoir, “And the Good News Is…: Lessons and Wisdom from the Bright Side of Life,” published in 2011 to widespread acclaim. Through her book, Perino shared valuable insights from her experiences, offering readers practical life lessons applicable to various situations.

Personal Life

In 1997, fate smiled upon Dana Perino as she crossed paths with her future husband, Peter McMahon, a native of England. This fortuitous encounter blossomed into a lasting connection, culminating in their marriage in 1998.

At 51 years old, Dana Perino has shared her life with Peter McMahon since their union in 1998. The couple’s story began during a chance meeting on a flight to Chicago in 1997. Despite their enduring companionship, the Fox television host Dana Perino and her husband, Peter McMahon, have not expanded their family with children.

Later on, in May of 2012, Perino was allowed to appear on Jeopardy! as part of its “Power Players” week. During this competition, she competed against notable individuals such as Kareem Abdul-Jabbar and David Faber from CNBC. 

Dana Perino, a prominent figure, has made Bay Head, New Jersey, her home. Throughout the years, she captured hearts and admiration for her cherished Vizsla companion, Jasper. Sadly, Jasper’s unexpected passing on September 4, 2021, brought profound sorrow to Dana and her husband, Peter. Despite the heartbreaking loss, Dana later shared on Fox News Channel that they welcomed a new Vizsla named Percy into their lives. This announcement brought solace to the grieving couple and their supporters, helping to heal the void left by Jasper’s departure.
